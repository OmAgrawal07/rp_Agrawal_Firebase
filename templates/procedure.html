Procedure


Step

Description

Image

1

Sterlize six bottles of water in the autoclave, filled with 490 mL of Poland Spring Water and 10 mL of Alga-Gro.

2

Add the sterlized water to the six different Veriner Biochamber 2000 tanks.

3

Add 1 gram of the cereal plant straws to their corresponding tanks. Four of the six tanks will have cereal straw.

4

Add 4 mL of the algae, Anabaena inaequalis, to five of the six tanks. The one tank that will not have any algae is one of the two tanks that did not have cereal plant straws.

5

Data collection begins with oxygen and carbon dioxide. The Vernier carbon dioxide sensor and Vernier oxygen sensor were attached to my laptop and then inserted into the tanks; they both ran for two minutes at the same time. The data collected was averaged and these trial were run for each tank once per week, for a total of seven weeks.

6

In the final weeks of data collection, absorbance readings were also recorded. The original algae sample was first used to determine the wavelength of light at which the algae absorbs the most light. The solutions were added to the cuvette and then inserted into the Vernier spectrophotometer to collect the absorbance readings.

7

The final week was left to look at qualititave data. The amount of algae in the tanks were recorded and then eventaully cleaned out.
